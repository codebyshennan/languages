Pronunciation Reference

A complete overview of Vietnamese tones, vowels, and consonants. Click 🔉 on any row to hear the example word via text-to-speech.

1. The 6 Tones

Vietnamese is a tonal language — the same syllable spoken with a different tone is an entirely different word. All six tones are demonstrated using syllables based on ma.

# Mark Name Pitch description Example 🔉
1 (none) ngang Mid level, steady ma
2 ` (grave) huyền Low falling, breathy mà
3 ? (hook) hỏi Mid dipping then rising mả
4 ~ (tilde) ngã Mid rising, creaky/glottal mã
5 ´ (acute) sắc High rising má
6 . (dot below) nặng Low falling, heavy/cut short mạ

2. Simple Vowels

Vietnamese has 12 simple vowels. Several use diacritics to distinguish them from similar sounds — the diacritics on vowels indicate vowel quality, not tone.

4. Initial Consonants

Vietnamese spelling uses several digraphs and letters with non-obvious pronunciations. Regional variation between Northern (Hanoi) and Southern (Ho Chi Minh City) dialects is significant.

5. Final Consonants

Key point: The stops -c, -ch, -p, -t are unreleased — there is no puff of air at the end. The nasals -ng and -nh nasalise the preceding vowel rather than being strongly articulated.
Spelling Type Notes Example 🔉
-c unreleased stop No audible release burst học
-ch unreleased stop Palatal — like -k but further forward sách
-m nasal Bilabial nasal cam
-n nasal Alveolar nasal can
-ng nasal Nasalises preceding vowel; velar nasal sang
-nh nasal Nasalises vowel; palatal nasal anh
-p unreleased stop Lips close but do not pop open hợp
-t unreleased stop Tongue contacts roof but no burst hát
